About

Mick Graham

  • 1982-1985

    Employed as a Lagger and Sheet Metal Worker

    Here I worked on the construction of unit 1 and unit 2 coal fired power stations in Port Augusta. My role was to install cladding and insulation on the turbine. I also completed work with steam pipes, fuel oil pipes, vessels and boilers.

  • 1985 - 2017

    Employed as Lead Handing/Supervisor for Thermal Cladding Companies

    My tasks included manufacturing and installing cladding and insulation on boiler walls, fans, steam pipes, fuel oil pipework, steam turbines, perforated cladding on drain lines and large scale ductwork. Additionally, I manufactured various corner and expansion joint flashings, tool boxes, pump covers, louvres and vent covers.

  • 2017 - Present

    Maintenance Worker at Sundrop Farms

    Currently working at Sundrop Farms on the maintence side.

  • Post 2017

    Initial Development of Sheet Metal Knights

    After the closure of the Northen and Playford powerstations, I took 6 months off before searching for new employment. During this time I began developing sheet metal knights which was something I always wanted to try. Through this I successfully manufactured several sheet metal knight sculptures, some of which are on display above. I want to continue improving my design, with the eventual aim of displaying them in South Australia as a business to put a smile on peoples faces.

What Am I Looking For?

I'm looking for someone to work with me as a business partner or mentor to create a medieval and rustic themed venue for people to hire. My plan is to display my sculptures in an old building which people can hire out for events such as birthday parties, conferences and social gatherings.
